Tunisia Tours

Yomadic Tunisia tours are for people who love the spirit of independent travel, and the camaraderie of a small group of travelling friends, but really wouldn’t enjoy a typical “corporate” group tour. Since 2014, Yomadic “untours” have combined the best aspects of freestyle travel, wrapped inside a curated (but flexible) itinerary.

These are the best available small-gang Tunisia tours, and we personally invite you to join us.

From Tunis, the busy Mediterranean capital once the heart of the Roman Empire in Africa, you’ll take an immersive journey into the Sahara and back. Situated between endless desert sands and crisp sea waters, millennia of civilizations have called Tunisia home – Cathegenians, Romans, Phoenicians, Spanish, Arabs, Ottomans – leading to a truly unique and contemporary blend of Europe and Africa.

Winding medinas, life-sustaining Oases, great food, friendly laid back people, and world-class reminders leftover from the clash of empires – there is so much to discover. Our local experience will lead you from iconic UNESCO sites to those special, locals-only, off-the-beaten-path places that don’t show on any other itinerary. Most importantly, you’ll have time to simply relax and let Tunisia come to you.

Yomadic Tunisia Tour Itinerary – Highlights

Clash of civilizations: explore the ruins of Carthage, Roman amphitheatres, Berber villages, holy Islamic sites, and taste the unique influences that have created the cuisine of modern Tunis.

From blue seas to sandy deserts: seaside cities and fishing villages, jagged hilltops and canyons, salt lakes, lush oases, and the worlds greatest desert.

A galaxy not that far away: you will feel like you’re on another planet, surrounded by the natural grandeur used as spectacular backdrops and set locations for many movies.

Sahara: your life under the stars, in the place that simply means “desert” – the Sahara.

BONUS: we minimise driving time and stay in centrally located accommodation. Each Yomadic Tunisia tour is unique – we’ll take advantage of any special events that may be happening when you visit. We keep a few secrets to not spoil the surprise, and you’ll see far more than the itinerary below.

 

Yomadic Tunisia Tour – Daily Itinerary

DAY 1 – WELCOME TO TUNISIA
  • Welcome to Tunis, after checking into your historical (and just a little bit fancy) hotel in the very heart of Tunis, you’ll soon realise all major points of interest are nearby. You may be a little tired from the flight, so a guided walking tour through a living labyrinth of history will be a relaxing way to discover this alluring capital city.
  • nearby Tunis Medina, a UNESCO World Heritage site, is a city-within-a-city – home to thousands of residents, market stalls, restaurants, and some of the most important Mosques in Tunisia. We’ll have time for an expert overview of what makes this city tick, and taste a little local flavour as we go.
  • get-to-know-you dinner will be at the best restaurant in Tunis (in our humble opinion)(and we’ll feel bad if you don’t allow us to pay)
Whilst in Tunis, we’ll spend two nights at a downtown historical hotel. Perfect location, comfortable rooms, inside an extensively restored classical building (with a great breakfast).
DAY 2 – LAYERS UPON LAYERS UPON LAYERS
  • Travel to the nearby Mediterranean coastline, discovering the oldest part of Tunis, and – another UNESCO World Heritage site – the city of Carthage. For centuries, Arch-nemesis of the Romans, and the birthplace of Hannibal – who famously journeyed his army of elephants into Europe. Carthage is varied – from the enormous Roman-era Cisterns of La Malga, to mysterious sites of infant sacrifice, and we’ll check out some of the more worthy spots.
  • speaking of the sea, after exploring Carthage we’ll head over to the beach-side town of La Goulette to eat. Fresh fish, grilled seafood, it’s a popular place for the locals and you’ll start to get under the skin of Tunis (but you won’t see any other tourists).
  • heading back to Tunis, we’ll explore the modern-day bustling city, taking in some of the secrets, and ending the day with dinner. You may want to explore the nearby medina on your own, after all, the Tunis Medina holds a lifetime of discoveries (we’ll be there to assist with any guidance and suggestions). We’d love to show you some quirky highlights (hashtag brutalist architecture).
Tonight is your second night in the little-bit-fancy downtown historical hotel.
DAY 3 – TUNIS TO KAIROUAN
  • Leaving Tunis, we’ll hit the road early and stop at Sousse – a busy port city in the Gulf of Hammamet. At its core, the millennia-old narrow streets of Sousse are a great demonstration of Tunisian architectural diversity, so we’ll wander, explore, take in some fortified history (with rooftop views), and check out the most incredible Roman-era mosaics.
  • Wandering around is the perfect way to see Sousse, and build up an appetite – and our reward will be some more of that great Tunisian cuisine.
  • by the afternoon, we’ll arrive in the small city of Kairouan. Another change of era’s – Kairouan is one of the most important Islamic places on Earth. Local highlights include the UNESCO listed Grand Mosque, offbeat must-sees include the place where Muhammad’s barber is laid to rest (yes, that Muhammad). Relax, explore, or even (clears throat) just rock the local Kasbah.
Tonight, we’ll spend a night inside the Kasbah, at a five-star hotel that fuses ancient traditions with contemporary standards (in Tunisia even the accommodations are highlights).
DAY 4 – EL DJEM TO DJERBA
  • Heading deeper into the South of Tunisia (queue Banjo music), but first, we’ll check out yet another UNESCO World Cultural Heritage site. OK, by this point, it may seem that UNESCO hands out these designations like candy – with little ol’ Tunisia containing eight. However, the Amphitheatre of El Jem is ridiculously worthy – unique in Africa, and one of the largest, best-preserved, Roman amphitheatres in the world.
  • en-route destinations today include the Aghlabid Basins – a grand hydraulic structure built by the Arab dynasty in the 9th century, providing water to the people of Kairouan for over a millennium. Plus, we have another surprise (or two).
  • after exploring El Jem, we’ll continue to Djerba – often referred to as “the Island of Dreams”. Catching a leisurely ferry is the perfect way to enter the laid-back Tunisian Mediterranean island. Rich with both history and contemporary arts – known for the ancient Jewish community near “Djerbahood”, where 250 artists from all around the world have left their works on the city streets.
  • Ending the day we’ll relax, eat, and spend the night on Djerba island.
Our accommodation in Djerba will be in a traditional, and quiet, boutique guest house.
DAY 5 – LEAVING DJERBAHOOD, ENTERING TATAOUINE
  • Djerbahood is best explored on foot, and we’ll visit some local highlights including the historic Jewish Synagogue – architecturally stunning, with deep religious importance. On the walls of Djerbahood, the contemporary artworks are everywhere – so we are unlikely to run out of photo opportunities.
  • let’s be honest- cheesy package tourists love Djerba. If you want to spend a week in a typical seaside resort, not seeing anything else, Djerba is for you. However, we like to educate and amuse, so we have a very special resort just for you…
  • Tanit Hotel resort is completely abandoned, beautifully decaying – and happens to be located on the sands of Tunisia’s nicest beach. We’ll stop in, check it out, learn about Tunisia’s “Jasmine Revolution” the 2011 civil protest that ended decades of corruption, sparked the Arab Spring, and Tanit Hotel is a perfect reminder of the economic turmoil that followed. Listen, take a few photos of the resort, or take a dip in some truly stunning warm waters – we’ll wait.
  • for lunch, Djerba has a lively Medina with a special twist – fisherman string up their catch, and display it to curious buyers. We’ll select what we need, and ask a local restaurant to grill up our “catch”, served with fresh salads and Tunisian sides.
  • in the afternoon, we’ll cross the first of our Sahara-salt-lakes, head into the beginnings of the real desert, and arrive in Tataouine.
  • after a long day of adventure, we think a BBQ by the pool would be perfect, before nodding off and sleeping in another galaxy far, far, away.
In Tataouine, for two nights everyone has a comfortable stone-house in the shadow of the rocky mountains.
DAY 6 – HILLTOP HOODS
  • Today is all about Tataouine. You’ll see the variety of dwellings and lifestyles of the locals – from hilltop caves to below-ground dugouts. It’s a full day of exploring, and we’ll see the best of the best. We’ll ask the locals to prepare a traditional Berber meal for us, and stop for a cup of tea and a snack inside the quaint cave of some local friends
  • Luke Skywalkers house is around here – Star Wars fan or not, this area is among the most visually stunning areas you will ever see. Tataouine was good enough for one of the most famous Hollywood Directors of all time, so our photos should look great.
  • up in the mountains is “Chenini” an ancient Berber village tucked into the cliffs, former “Ksar” (fortified storage facility), and OK, yes Star Wars nerds we know Chenini is also the name of one of the moons on Luke Skywalker’s home planet.
  • after a full day of exploring, we’ll return to our hotel and get some rest for tomorrow’s journey into the greatest desert on Earth.
Your second night in Tataouine, inside your comfortable stone-house (the pool is a short walk away).
DAY 7 – SAHARA MEANS DESERT
  • Up over the mountains (stopping for coffee with an amazing view), we’ll spend the day travelling into the Sahara desert, and we’ll need to swap our comfortable bus for a set of off-road equipped vehicles. Along the way, we have a few surprises – some above ground, some below. We know a hot-water desert-spring where the wild camels will usually be hanging out drinking.
  • lunch, we’re planning on eating inside a traditional troglodytes home (the temperature is always perfect, and the not-expected interior decorating could be described as “Grandma-ish”)
  • heading off-road and into the desert, we’ll arrive in the middle of nowhere – sixty miles from the nearest human settlement, and thirty miles from the closest source of water (don’t worry, we’ll bring more than enough).
  • finally, we will reach our destination – our campsite in the Sahara (with enough all-terrain-vehicles to share and explore some of the more remote desert sites).
  • by night, we’ll enjoy a traditional Berber dinner (and of course a desert camp-fire).
  • stargazing after a good meal will take your breath away. With zero light pollution (since the closest source of electricity is two hours drive away from our camp), you’ll witness the galaxy with a clarity you may not have believed is possible.
Our accommodations for tonight are comfortable desert camp tents, but feel free to sleep out under the stars.
DAY 8 – TOZEUR TREEHOUSE
  • Leaving the Sahara desert in the morning, we’ll cross the vast salt lake of Chott el Djerid. Shimmering with hues of green, red and white, we’ll take the one narrow road that allows us to cross safely (stopping in the middle to check out the pink salt lake), before continuing on to the small town of Douz – just a stone’s throw from the Algerian border.
  • Douz is small and lively and makes for a perfect lunch stop.
  • there is a seriously unique stop en-route today, the Tunisian-only Les Refroidisseurs d’Eau – a huge concrete facility built to process, cool, and store, the hot water that flows up from underground the desert sands. This doesn’t appear on any other itineraries – only the locals know about this location (the perfect place to enjoy a free, hot, mineral-water shower).
  • along the way, we’ll stop for photos and stop for breaks before continuing on to Tozeur. A small town, known for it’s exceptional and unique methods of cooking lamb (these next two dinners will be memorable, believe it).
Would you like your very own tree-house style apartment, with no neighbours, built on stilts, in the middle of a tropical Orchard-Oasis? OK, you can stay for two nights, but you may want to never leave.
DAY 9 – JUST A BIT SALTY
  • Today we will again head off-road, inside 4WD vehicles right across the vast salt lake and desert sands. Stopping for picturesque views, you will truly feel like you’re in the middle of nowhere. There’s a special visit to the famous Mos Espa townsite, decaying in the middle of the desert. You’ll also see a surprising amount of lush scenery in the area – including Oases and bountiful date-farms.
  • back in Tozeur, we’ll have lunch in the middle of our day trip, and check out the local Medina, Souk, and Tozeur’s unique architecture.
  • You may need a break around now, after all, you have a tree-house to get back to. However, we have an optional excursion – we would love to show you Lars Homestead (those who know, know, and those who don’t know, you should think about joining in for the journey, we’ll have a few laughs).

Night two in your tree-house apartment, in the middle of a tropical Orchard-Oasis.
DAY 10 – CONQUERING SBEITLA
  • There’s a journey going on today, as we head north along the Algerian border, from Tozeur to the ancient city of Sbeitla. Historically significant, also known as “Sufetula”, Sbeitla is where the Muslim conquest of Africa first began – changing the culture of the continent until this day. BTW, did we mention that “Africa” was originally the name of the area now named Tunisia?
  • Arriving in Sbeitla, we’ll have time to explore some local Roman ruins, and have wild-boar for lunch, as was the style of the time back in Ancient Africa.
  • by night, after a day of walking, exploring, and driving, we’ll relax before waking up for our one final full day in Tunisia.
Accommodation tonight is at a four-star hotel, the best beds for hours in any direction.
DAY 11 – WHEN IN ROME
  • Dougga is the best-preserved Roman town in Africa, and perhaps anywhere on the planet. An archaeologists paradise, and recognised by UNESCO as such, we’ll explore the ancient temples, bath-houses, incredibly preserved Roman mosaics, and the original Roman streets. It’s a huge, exceptional, and perhaps unparalleled slice of history. Streets, buildings, tunnels, temples, forums, Dougga covers 160 acres and includes the remains of countless structures (with ever more being uncovered).
  • You’ll be a Tunisia-expert by this point, and will realise there simply isn’t enough time to see everything. However, we have a few “stand-bys” if we have extra time on our drive back to Tunis city – completing our loop of this country.

Is Tunisia Safe?

Forget the media, forget what you’ve heard from people who have never visited, Tunisia is probably safer than the country you live in. Tunisians are friendly, hospitable, and welcoming, they’ll chat with you on the streets, and tourists are treated with respect – by the locals and the authorities. As with many countries around the world, Tunisia has in the past sadly experienced acts of terrorism. However, these were limited in scope, or solo isolated incidents. Tunisia is a stable democratic nation, a beacon of Africa, with no ongoing conflicts.
